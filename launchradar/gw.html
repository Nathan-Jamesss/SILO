Grants & Accelerators for Early-Stage Founders

  • General

Below is a running list of grants, programs and accelerators that can provide capital, mentorship, or community at the earliest stages. We’ll keep it updated as new opportunities emerge.

Have one to add? Email us.

Upcoming Deadlines:

  • a16z / Speedrun: Every part of Speedrun is focused on helping you move fast and build a generational company. Join to get: Capital and credits, a launch platform and the opportunity to join an elite community. Apply by May 17
  • Black Venture Institute: BLCK VC’s marquee program designed to prepare Black professionals for angel investing, scouting, and venture investing opportunities. Apply by May 18
  • Blue Startups: a Top 20 Accelerator in the US (Techcrunch) focused on helping scalable-technology companies compete globally. Blue Startups aims to stimulate economic growth in Hawaii and create new business opportunities by providing promising entrepreneurs with mentorship, access to business expertise, product testing, peer review, pitch development, introductions to investors, and seed funding.
  • Cartier Womens Initiative: The Cartier Women’s Initiative is an annual international entrepreneurship program that aims to drive change by empowering women impact entrepreneurs. Founded in 2006, the program is open to women-run and women-owned businesses from any country and sector that aim to have a strong and sustainable social and/or environmental impact. Apply by June 16
  • Entrepreneurship World Cup:  A global expansion opportunity for entrepreneurs across idea, early and growth stages, with substacks for companies leveraging AI. Top applicants will have access to bootcamps, mentorship and a chance for a share or $1,000,000 in equity-free cash prizes. Apply by May 31
  • Founders of the Future: Where the next generation of transatlantic founders win up to £1.7 million in investment, alongside the connections and momentum that define their next chapter. Apply by April 30
  • Google for startups: Each cohort of 10-15 startups comes together to tackle technical challenges that can help grow their businesses through a mix of remote and in-person, 1-to-1, group learning sessions, and sprint projects. Apply by June 30
  • Helion House: Launching in Phoenix and Denver in September 2026, the program begins with a 6-month, execution-first experience, with extensions for the highest-signal builders. Helion is built around one standard — real companies with real revenue — with additional pathways into high-growth startups or further education backed by proven execution. Residents gain access to a network of founders, operators, and investors, along with curated tools, capital pathways, and direct exposure to real-world building. Interested candidates can learn more and join the waitlist. Final cohorts will be announced on June 15, 2026
  • Impact Catalyst: a seed-stage, non-dilutive funding program designed for early-stage life science startups. It addresses funding gaps in underinvested areas aligned with disease burden and accelerates innovation in hard-to-commercialize areas across Massachusetts.  Apply by June 12
  • The Famous Amos Ingredients for Success: Annual national award program launched by Ferrero North America in 2020. IFS creates pathways for early-stage Black business owners to thrive by providing $150,000 in capital awards, mentorship, networking, and educational resources. Apply byJune 2
  • LIT Summit 2026: This global virtual experience brings together engineers, founders, marketers, product leaders, recruiters, and rising professionals for a full day of powerful conversations, practical workshops, and meaningful connections. May 21
  • LBAN Startup Accelerator: Stanford Graduate School of Business will help startups establish business principles that accelerate growth and traction. This is a 9-week hybrid program. Upcoming Cohort: March 27 through May 31, 2026
  • MassNextGen: Awardees of the program will receive a year-long customized package of support, which includes non-dilutive grant funding, lab bench at a sponsoring incubator, and access to a network of seasoned professionals from the life sciences ecosystem helping them to refine their business strategies and effectively raise capital. In addition, awardees will highlight their company to sponsors, awardees, and representatives of venture capital firms at the end of the program year in a “showcase” event to promote relationship building. Apply by June 12
  • Oregon Innovation Showcase:  brings together investors, entrepreneurs, and ecosystem leaders to highlight Oregon as a premier hub for innovation and startups. Focusing on deep tech and innovation-driven companies, the Showcase connects emerging startups with investors, celebrates Oregon-grown success stories, and spotlights the state’s supportive ecosystem that helps de-risk investments and drive scalable growth. May 11 – 12
  • Pennsylvania Small Business Advantage Grant: The Small Business Advantage Grant provides reimbursement grants ranging from 50% to 80% of eligible project costs to Pennsylvania small businesses to improve energy efficiency or reduce pollution or waste. Project work for eligible projects and applicants must start on or after July 1, 2025, and must be complete by June 30, 2026.
  • Portland Startup Week: Driven by founders, builders, and champions across the region, the week brings together gatherings that spark learning, connection, and momentum. May 11 – 16
  •  Principled Business Pitch Competition: pre-seed and seed-stage founders from industries ranging from AI and energy to crypto, consumer products, and more. This competition will showcase innovative solutions to an audience of investors, executives, and industry leaders committed to advancing free markets. Apply by June 2
  • Startup Battlefield 200: The world’s most promising early-stage startups. Selected by TechCrunch. Trained, tested, and put on a global stage. Startup Battlefield brings together 200 standout companies to showcase at Disrupt and compete for a $100K equity-free prize, investor attention, and the kind of media exposure that can change the trajectory of a company overnight. Nominate yourself, a fellow founder, or a portfolio company by May 27th
  • Village Capital the Future of Aging 2026: Supporting entrepreneurs building solutions that strengthen the social and economic conditions for aging with dignity, agency, and connection. Apply by May 17
  • Women Founders Network: Women Founders Network 14th Annual Fast Pitch Competition will open applications for the 2026 season on April 1. The winner gets a $25k cash grant. Apply by May 31
  • Y Combinator: accepting applications for the Summer 2026 Batch funding cycle. The batch will take place from July to September in San Francisco. You can also apply to future batches (Fall, Winter, and Spring) The deadline to apply on-time is May 4 at 8pm PT

Rolling Deadlines and Resources:

  • .Tech Domains: get the best name for your startup on .tech. Sponsored for 10 years.
  • 500 Global Flagship Accelerator: Startups and small businesses can apply year-round. This program offers businesses the opportunity for a four-month accelerator program based in San Francisco, along with a significant investment.
  • AI2 Incubator: offers up to $500K in founder-friendly pre-seed funding and 12–18 months of deep technical support to AI-first startups. Backed by the Allen Institute for AI, the program includes cofounder matching, $1M+ in compute credits, expert product design, cutting-edge research access, and quarterly in-person weeks in Seattle.
  • AfroTech Conference: AFROTECH™ Conference is a premier annual event at the intersection of technology, culture, and innovation. While rooted in uplifting Black professionals in tech and beyond, AFROTECH™ is a space where people of all backgrounds come together to celebrate Black excellence, share knowledge, spark innovation, and build meaningful connections
  • Amber Grants: awards up to $10K monthly grants to women founders; rolling applications.
  • Blue Startups Cohort 18: Blue Startups has a proven track record of empowering early-stage startups, providing them with invaluable resources, mentorship, and access to a vibrant entrepreneurial ecosystem. Apply by May 15
  • CO—100 by the U.S. Chamber of Commerce: Honors the top 100 small businesses making an outsized impact on the American economy, with a $25,000 grand prize, includes a special category for Tech Innovators recognizing companies using new technologies to scale, enhance customer experience, or innovate. 
  • Dream Ventures Accelerator: offers lifetime access to 6 self-paced fundraising modules, private resource library, a 1:1 strategy session with Annie Evans, investor pitch competition, weekly live office hours, founder accountability pods, and a NYC celebration dinner; $1,700 enrollment fee.
  • ElevenLabs Start up Grants:  The ElevenLabs Grants Program exists to give early-stage startups and small teams the tools they need to build the future of AI audio with the best tools available, without cost being a blocker. SF IRL has partnered with ElevenLabs to give founders 33 million in ElevenLabs credits or equivalent to $4k to build voice AI agents. Use code 11SFIRL723 for expedited review.
  • Entrepreneurs First Residency: Whether you’re ready to go all in or still exploring ideas, residencies in San Francisco give you 3 months to build, experiment, and find the right cofounder. They accept applications on a rolling basis.
  • Forum Ventures Accelerator: offers $100K for 7.5% to early B2B SaaS startups with a 16-week remote program focused on growth, fundraising, and AI/health/fintech support.
  • Founders, Inc.: offers $250K for 3–5% to emerging tech startups, along with a long-term SF campus membership. No pitch decks—just apply to build alongside top builders in AI, Web3, AR/VR, and more.
  • Founder Institute: Network of startup incubators, accelerators, and investors. Our mission is to activate entrepreneurial potential and empower communities across the globe to build ventures with positive economic and societal impact.
  • Freed Fellowship: Every month, we select a business owner in the US as a Freed Fellow. Each Fellow receives a $500 grant to invest in their existing business. No strings attached. No equity required.
  • GanasVentures: Ganas Fund I invests $100K into pre-seed and seed community-driven startups building a better future across the US & Latin America. Fund II will invest globally.
  • HerRise Microgrant: offers financial support to innovative women who struggle to secure funding for their community-impacting small businesses. Monthly applications.
  • Hubspot’s The Hustle: No matter what stage of the founder journey you’re on right now, we know a little free press couldn’t hurt.Tell us all about your growing biz for a chance to have your startup featured in The Hustle, and connect with our 1.5m readers.
  • KDD Startup Studio: is now accepting applications for its next founder cohort, launching in early 2026. The program is built for driven individuals who want to start companies from the ground up. No startup idea is required to apply. KDD supports founders by matching them with co-founders and providing structured guidance to help teams go from zero to one.
  • Living Lab Open Call for Smart City Solutions: Downtown Brooklyn Partnership (DBP) invites technology companies and startups to pilot innovative solutions in our public spaces. 
  • The Lynx List: offers investors weekly spotlights on 1–2 NYC pre-seed/seed startups actively fundraising—with a short founder interview video and memo-style write-up.
  • The Pitch by Deel: Founders: apply for a $1M investment on a rolling deadline
  • Peachscore + Dealum Accelerator: the world’s largest data-driven accelerator platform offers rolling admissions and instant AI-led application reviews. Virtual and industry-agnostic, it supports startups from idea to Series A with $5M+ in perks, investor exposure to 30,000+ backers, and the ability to start in the same month you apply.
  • Pegasus Angel Accelerator: A startup accelerator focused on connecting you to what matters most when scaling a venture— people.
  • RCF Social Impact Grant: offers $15K non-dilutive grants to social-impact founders; rolling applications.
  • Right Side Capital 2026 Pre-Seed & Seed Active VC List: personally curated list of around 200 venture capitalists who actively invest at the Pre-Seed and Seed stages.
  • Santander US Grant Program: offers $20K grants to East Coast founders; 2025 application details forthcoming.
  • Small Business Improvement Fund: The Small Business Improvement Fund (SBIF) provides reimbursement grant funding for permanent building improvements and repairs across Chicago. Applications are submitted during monthly rollout periods for individual TIF districts.
  • Secretsos small business grant: This quarterly grant program provides $2,500 in funding to help established businesses overcome challenges and fuel growth. Recipients have complete flexibility in how they use the funds, whether for hiring, operations, or implementing new strategies. 
  • Startup Leadership Program: Fellowship for first-time founders to become lifetime entrepreneurs.
  • Soma Fellowship: offers up to $1M on an uncapped SAFE to early-stage founders, with potential follow-on funding of $10M+. Backed by Soma Capital, the program provides access to a powerful network of unicorn founders, investors, and mentors to help build the next generation of iconic companies.
  • StartOut Growth Lab Accelerator: making a real impact promoting diversity within startup communities across the nation. StartOut Growth Lab (“SGL”) hosts a new virtual cohort of early-stage companies every six months.
  • Startup Battlefield 200 Masterclass: VC Unfiltered: Candid Advice to Help You Land the Check
  • Techstars: Join Techstars next spring and accelerate your company with:$220,000 improved investment offer to fuel your growth.
  • TechRise Chicago Grants: awards $25K – $50K non-dilutive funding to Chicago founders via weekly pitch competitions; rolling applications.
  • VC Match: Upload your pitch deck and get instantly matched with investors who are actively looking for companies like yours
  • VU’s Venture Capital Investor Accelerator: cohort members “learn by doing” inside a top-performing VC fund (VU Venture Partners), and work alongside and receive mentorship from senior fund managers
  • Warrior Rising Grant: Up to $20K for veteran entrepreneurs. Rolling deadline. 
  • Women Founder Grants: $5,000 GrantAwarded on a rolling basis.
  • Y Startup School: Learn how to start a company, with help from the world’s top startup accelerator – Y Combinator. Startup School is a free online course on how to start a startup.

  • Diversify: An extraordinary celebration at San Francisco Museum Of Modern Art showcasing 200+ current female founders from Bay Area & Boston hosted by Daxe. May 6

  • Power to Pitch HSFS Grant: HubSpot for Startups and Kat Weaver are teaming up to fund one early-stage founder with a $2,500 grant — and the spotlight to match. If you’re building, testing, selling, and figuring it out in real time, this is for you. Apply by May 6th
  • Project Female Founder: Global accelerator program, unlocking opportunities for female entrepreneurs to scale their ventures in fintech and enterprise technology.
